At each moment, light (as well as electromagnetism and gravitational force) is coming towards you from every direction, in a sphere around you.\u00c2\u00a0 The light has left its location at different times, from the Moon; 2 seconds ago, the Sun; 8 minutes ago, Vega; 25 years ago, Andromeda; 2 million years ago and so on.\u00c2\u00a0 You could imagine a set of concentric spheres, each one containing a part of the universe you are experiencing.\u00c2\u00a0 The greater the radius of the sphere, the farther back in time you experience that part.\u00c2\u00a0 In the diagram this is represented as the \u00e2\u20ac\u0153past light cone<\/a>\u00e2\u20ac\u009d; each \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcsphere\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 becomes a circular slice of that cone.\u00c2\u00a0 The present moment of each location in spacetime is the focused intersection or standing wave pattern<\/a> of all waves and forces originating within the past light cone of that location.\u00c2\u00a0 Likewise, all electromagnetic and gravitational waves and forces traveling into the future of the present are \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcdispersed\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 relative to the observer by the inverse square law; intensity is proportional to 1\/(distance\u00c2\u00b2).\u00c2\u00a0 For example, all the light the bounced off of a pterosaur<\/a><\/span> 200 million years ago and passed through Earths atmosphere is still out there, traveling, 200 million light years away.\u00c2\u00a0 It is incredibly diffuse, you would need to construct a telescope billions of light years across to create an image, but the information still exists.\u00c2\u00a0 <\/span><\/p>\n

For forces that travel at light speed, we only experience and effect the surface of the light cones.\u00c2\u00a0 Matter, which travels at less than light speed through space, influences us and is effected by us within the volume of the light cones.\u00c2\u00a0 All matter and forces travel at light speed, but in different dimensions.<\/strong>\u00c2\u00a0 Light is restricted to space dimensions, and does not experience time.\u00c2\u00a0 Matter travels at light speed primarily through time, and can also travel through space relative to other objects.\u00c2\u00a0 To maintain light speed, matter will move less through time, giving rise to relativistic effects such as time dilation.\u00c2\u00a0 Here I will cite “The Fabric of the Cosmos” by Brian Greene,<\/a><\/span> an excellent book that shaped my thinking on this subject.
<\/span><\/p>\n

The implications of light cones<\/a> are incredible.\u00c2\u00a0 At each moment your body is being influenced by the volume of the entire universe<\/strong>, in a gradient from moments ago to the big bang.\u00c2\u00a0 The size of the effect is mediated by the inverse square law, so the closest things effect you the most.\u00c2\u00a0 However if one star in the andromeda galaxy, 2 million years ago, disappeared, there would be a minute change in the gravitational pull on the atoms of your body. Perhaps a salt ion in one of your neurons wouldn\u00e2\u20ac\u2122t exit an ion channel, and your thoughts and life would be different.\u00c2\u00a0 This effect goes forward into time as well, every action we take effects everything in out future light cone.\u00c2\u00a0 As beings, we are completely integrated into the Universe, <\/strong>composed and created by every event in our past light cone, and manifesting everything in our future light cone.
