Graduate students at private universities are asking regulators to consider these questions: Are we employees, or not? Can we join a union?<\/p>\n

The National Labor Relations Board recently decided to review its previous position, reigniting debate within the ivory tower.<\/p>\n

For Paul Katz, who\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s three years into a history Ph.D. program at Columbia University, the 15 to 20 hours a week he spends teaching university undergraduates should mean he\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s an employee. He teaches in addition to conducting his own research.<\/p>\n

\u00e2\u20ac\u0153I grade all of their papers, their exams, I run review sessions, I meet with them one-on-one, basically support them throughout the semester,\u00e2\u20ac\u009d Katz says. \u00e2\u20ac\u0153The university should treat me as an employee because I do work for them.\u00e2\u20ac\u009d<\/p>\n
